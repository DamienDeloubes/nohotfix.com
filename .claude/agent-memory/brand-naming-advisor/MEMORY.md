## 0Hotfix / NoHotfix / 0Surprises / NoSurprises — Key Findings (researched May 2026)

- **NoHotfix**: No competing products, trademarks, or social handles found. Both nohotfix.com and nohotfix.com AVAILABLE. "Hotfix" is technical jargon — makes the composite mark suggestive, not merely descriptive. LOW risk. RECOMMENDED.
- **0Hotfix**: Identical conflict profile to NoHotfix. Both 0hotfix.com and 0hotfix.io AVAILABLE. Weaker on brand quality — digit friction in verbal/written communication. LOW risk but inferior to NoHotfix.
- **NoSurprises**: No competing SaaS product found. nosurprises.io AVAILABLE but nosurprises.com TAKEN since 2000-04-06 (GoDaddy, triple registrar locks — deliberate long-term defensive hold). Federal No Surprises Act (healthcare) dominates search results — permanent SEO pollution, especially damaging given healthtech ICP. "No surprises" is common everyday English → HIGH descriptiveness/non-registrability risk at USPTO. MEDIUM overall.
- **0Surprises**: No competing SaaS product found. 0surprises.com and 0surprises.io BOTH AVAILABLE. Avoids .com conflict vs NoSurprises. But: same weak aspirational phrase, same No Surprises Act SEO pollution, same USPTO descriptiveness problem, plus digit friction. MEDIUM overall.
- **Ranking (1=best)**: 1. NoHotfix, 2. 0Hotfix, 3. 0Surprises, 4. NoSurprises
- **Key insight**: "Hotfix" stems are more defensible than "surprise" stems because "hotfix" is industry-specific vocabulary that gives USPTO registration a foothold. "Surprise/no surprises" is everyday English with no technical specificity.
- **nosurprises.com note**: 25-year registration + triple clientDelete/Renew/Transfer locks = not a squatter, likely a real brand intent. Not acquirable without negotiation.
